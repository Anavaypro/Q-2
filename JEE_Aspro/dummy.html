Physics Medium 4 pts

Question:

A particle moves along the x-axis with velocity \( v = k\sqrt{x} \), where k is a constant. If at \( t = 0 \), \( x = 0 \), find the displacement \( x \) as a function of time \( t \).

Solution:

Given: \( v = \frac{dx}{dt} = k\sqrt{x} \)

Separate variables: \( \frac{dx}{\sqrt{x}} = k dt \)

Integrate both sides: \( 2\sqrt{x} = kt + C \)

At \( t = 0 \), \( x = 0 \), so \( C = 0 \)

Thus: \( \sqrt{x} = \frac{kt}{2} \)

Squaring both sides: \( x = \frac{k^2 t^2}{4} \)

Correct Answer: Option 2 (\( x = \frac{k^2 t^2}{4} \))

Kinematics Problem

May 15, 2023 20 min read

Question: A projectile is fired from the surface of the earth with a velocity of \( 5 \, \text{ms}^{-1} \) at an angle of \( 30^\circ \) with the horizontal. Another projectile is fired from the same point with the same speed but at a different angle. If the horizontal ranges are same for both, what is the ratio of maximum heights?

Detailed Solution:

For a projectile with initial velocity \( u \) and angle \( \theta \):

Range \( R = \frac{u^2 \sin 2\theta}{g} \)

Maximum height \( H = \frac{u^2 \sin^2 \theta}{2g} \)

Given two projectiles with angles \( \theta \) and \( 90^\circ - \theta \) will have same range.

Thus, ratio of maximum heights:

\[ \frac{H_1}{H_2} = \frac{\sin^2 \theta}{\sin^2 (90^\circ - \theta)} = \frac{\sin^2 \theta}{\cos^2 \theta} = \tan^2 \theta \]

For \( \theta = 30^\circ \), ratio is \( \tan^2 30^\circ = \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{3} \)
